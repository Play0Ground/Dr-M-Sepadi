ABOUT DR. MERCY SEPADI

Dr. Mercy Sepadi is the first PhD graduate in Public Health at the University of Johannesburg. She became the youngest Environmental Health Practitioner with a PhD in South Africa when she was conferred the degree on the 4th of April 2024 by the University of Johannesburg. Currently, she is a lecturer in the Department of Environmental Health at Tshwane University of Technology.

Dr Sepadi holds a National Diploma, a BTech in Environmental Health, a Master's, and a PhD in Public Health, all from the prestigious University of Johannesburg. She is a published author in international journals and an academic who supervises and mentors students from the honors to PhD levels. Dr. Sepadi's scholarly journey is marked by her relentless pursuit of knowledge and solutions in occupational health. Her research has unveiled the severe respiratory risks of informal miners and street vendors, a sector that had previously received little attention.

With 12 years of experience, Dr. Sepadi has worked in the private and public sectors. In 2021, she was promoted from Environmental Health Practitioner to Operations Manager at the City of Johannesburg before joining Tshwane University of Technology. In 2022, she was recognized as one of the Mail & Guardian's Top 200 Young South African Leaders, a significant honour and milestone in her career. Her research highlights include presentations at various international conferences and invitations from several media houses.

Dr. Sepadi believes that no one achieves greatness in isolation, success is a product of a supportive community. She emphasizes the importance of building a support network that serves as a sounding board and cheering squad.
